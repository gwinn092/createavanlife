# Overnight Van Power Checklist: The 60-Second Routine That Protects Tomorrow

Most next-day power stress starts the night before. Small habits compound overnight, especially during heat or weak-weather stretches.

**Mantra:** protect baseline, then sleep.

## The checklist (do this in order)

*(Trigger: run this checklist before you turn lights out.)*

- Set inverter state to match your overnight plan (default **OFF** unless AC is actively needed).
- Protect baseline loads first: fridge, essential ventilation, essential device access.
- Turn off optional comfort loads you don’t need overnight.
- Choose device charging intentionally (DC-first when practical).
- Confirm tomorrow’s recovery plan now (shore, driving, solar, or a combination).
- Do a quick weather reality check (heat, cloud cover, weak sun).
- Keep fan + fridge priorities clear before adding anything “extra.”
- Do a fast cable/cord tidy + safety check.
- Set a morning reminder: “check margin + recovery window.”
- Leave yourself a one-line note: **baseline-only** or **normal routine**.

## If tonight is hot

- Prioritize ventilation and fridge stability first.
- Trim optional loads earlier, not later.
- Keep overnight charging decisions intentional.
- Plan tomorrow’s first recovery window before sleep.

## If tomorrow looks cloudy

- Shift into conservation mode tonight.
- Protect baseline loads and trim convenience usage.
- Decide now where recovery comes from tomorrow (shore, driving, or both).
- Avoid late optional loads that borrow from weak-weather margin.

## If you’re plugged into shore power

- Use the window to recover intentionally, not impulsively.
- Avoid stacking surprise loads just because you’re plugged in.
- Prioritize baseline recovery before convenience usage.
- Before unplugging, decide the next recovery window and routine.

## FAQ

### Should I leave my inverter on overnight?
Usually only if AC is actively needed. Many van lifers default to off overnight to protect margin.

### What do I turn off first?
Turn off optional comfort loads first. Keep baseline loads protected.

### Can I charge devices overnight?
Yes, but do it intentionally. Use simple priorities and avoid stacking unnecessary loads.

### Why do I wake up with less margin than expected?
Small overnight drains add up, especially if optional loads and AC convenience are left running.

### What matters more: battery size or bedtime routine?
Both matter, but routine often determines whether a system feels stable day to day.

### How does this help during cloudy weeks?
It preserves margin so weak recovery days don’t compound as quickly.
