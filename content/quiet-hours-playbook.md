Quiet hours are easier when you treat them as a planning constraint, not a surprise. This playbook helps you protect recovery without rushing into bad purchases.

**Trust link:** **[How we test + how we label claims](/how-we-test-and-label-claims/)**.

## Start here

1. Pick your refill window before your loads stack up.
2. Choose a station that can recover within that window (Spec/Reported).
3. Keep one fallback move for bad-week drift.

## The playbook

### 1) Anchor around predictable windows
A repeatable one- to two-hour window often beats a bigger battery with slow refill.

### 2) Keep overnight loads deliberate
Protect the loads that keep comfort and safety stable; postpone optional loads until refill windows open.

### 3) Build a calm fallback
If one day misses, route to a fast recovery action instead of a panic upgrade.
