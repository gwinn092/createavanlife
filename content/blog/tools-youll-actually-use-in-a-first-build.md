The fastest way to overspend on a build is buying every tool recommended online.

## Buy First

A drill/driver combo earns its cost back faster than any other tool on the list. It's not just for the build — you'll use it for mounting, repairs, and adjustments for as long as you own the van. A cordless combo with two batteries is worth buying once and buying well. This is one of the few places where cheap is a false economy: a drill that strips screws or drops charge mid-session slows every task downstream.

A jigsaw isn't used daily, but it's hard to work around when you need it. Cutting fan holes in the roof, trimming flooring around wheel wells, fitting panels to irregular shapes — these are one-time cuts that need to be right. Renting a jigsaw works in theory, but tool rental timing rarely lines up with building momentum. Having it available for two weekends of active cutting justifies the cost.

A crimper and multimeter are the protection tools of the electrical side. Crimped connections hold; twisted-wire-and-tape connections loosen with road vibration over thousands of miles. A multimeter catches wiring problems before they become safety problems. Neither is expensive, and both are non-negotiable before you energize anything.

PPE and measuring tools — safety glasses, a tape measure, a speed square — are cheap and always necessary. No experienced builder skips them.

## Borrow or Rent

A miter saw is the first thing to borrow. It's the right tool for cutting furring strips and trim to length, and those cuts mostly happen during one or two focused build sessions. Buying a miter saw for a single conversion is real money spent on something you'll need to store or sell afterward. Borrow it for the weekend you need it.

A router falls in the same category. Useful for edge profiles and specialty joinery, rarely needed more than a few times in a first build. If someone you know has one, borrow it for a weekend. If not, design around straight edges and save the budget for things you'll actually use repeatedly.

## The Honest Truth About Tool Lists

Most online tool lists exist to generate affiliate clicks, not to give accurate guidance. A list of twenty-five tools generates twenty-five opportunities to earn commissions. The reality of a first van build is that about six or seven tools do ninety percent of the actual work. The rest sit in the corner.

The useful filter is simple: "Will I use this more than twice during the build?" If the honest answer is maybe, borrow it first. If you can't borrow it, rent it. Only buy what you'll reach for repeatedly. Build the habit of asking that question before every tool purchase, and you'll keep the budget tight without missing anything that actually matters.
